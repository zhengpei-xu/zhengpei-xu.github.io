I am an MRes Urban Spatial Science student at [The Bartlett Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis (CASA)](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/casa), University College London.

My research interests centre on understanding urban spatial patterns and dynamics, with a particular focus on how individual behaviour, infrastructure, and environmental processes interact to shape cities. 

I am also especially interested in combining data-driven spatial modelling, including machine learning approaches, and simulation-based methods to support spatial inference and policy-relevant urban analysis.

Current Focus
=======
I am currently working on two ongoing research projects.

- agent-based modelling of taxi cruising behaviour and congestion dynamics in Manhattan.
- machine learning–based spatial classification of urban patterns based in London.

Research Trajectory
======
In the longer term, I aim to pursue doctoral research in urban spatial science, urban simulation, or computational urban studies.

I am particularly interested in research environments that integrate quantitative modelling, simulation-based analysis, and policy-relevant urban research.

Opportunities
======
I am open to research assistant and collaboration opportunities related to urban modelling, spatial analysis, and simulation-based research.
